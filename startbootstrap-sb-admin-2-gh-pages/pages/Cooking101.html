  • Be aware of what you're doing. You are flammable.
  • Sharpen your knives! It's safer since it'll be easier to cut things.

Basic Knife Classifications

To become more effective in the kicthen, you should know the proper uses of each knife. This will not only prolong the life of your knives but would also prevent injuries. Below you can find a quick guide to the everyday essential kives.

Paring knife

A paring knife is a good all-around knife. It is ideally a sharp blade often used for chopping small items like fruit and herbs. During apple pie season, it really comes in handy when peeling all those apples.

Chef's knife

Similar to the paring knife, a chef's knife is a multipurpose knife in the kitchen. It is approximately 8 to 10 inches long. A chef's knife rounded tip makes it ideal for rock chopping and its straight edge is ideal for tap chopping. For most people, the chef knife is the most used knife in the kitchen.

Bread Knife

A bread knife, as the name implies, is most useful when slicing bread. Bread knives are also useful when slicing pineapple! Definitely keep this beautiful tool in mind during the summer months.

Santoku Knife

A Santoku knife is very similar in use as the chef's knife. The Santoku knife is really usefule when slicing and dicing in the kicthen. This knife originated from Japan and is very efficient.

Plastic vs Wood cutting boards

First, to anyone who is in doubt, yes you need a cutting board. Do NOT cut on your countertop. The kind of cutting board you use affects the sanitation process of your board. It could also affect the lifetime of your knife.

Plastic Cutting Boards

Plastic cutting boards are cheaper than wooden cutting boards. They are easier to clean than their wooden counterpart. Thus, many would argue that they are cleaner than the other kinds of cutting board. However, some people fail to realize that plastic cutting boards tend to be ridden with gashes from knife cuts. Bacteria can grow within these gashes, thereby contamonating the board itself.

Wooden Cutting Boards

Wooden cutting boards tend to be more expensive but lasts longer than plastic cutting boards. Depending on the kind of wood that the board is made off, the lifetime of your knife and board can vary. Cutting boards made out of hardwoods like walnut and maple will dull your knife faster but will be more resistant to scratches and cuts. On the other hand, boards made out of softer woods wold dull your knife less but could sustain cuts faster. Just like in the plastic boards, bacteria grows within the cuts in the wood.

Cleaning your boards

Because plastic and wood boards have different characteristics, their care instrcution would also be different. Plastic boards can be put in the dishwasher and cleaned with high temperature water. On the other hand, wood cutting boards will be ruined by the dishwasher

If cleaning boards by hand, whether it is plastic or wood, you should do the following:

  • Rinse the board of debris
  • Scrubb the board with soap and water
  • Rinse the board thoroughly
  • Sanitize with the appropriate sanitizer
    • Wood cutting boards should be sanitized using an quaternary ammonium sanitizer.
    • Plastic boards should be sanitized using a chlorine-based soluton.
